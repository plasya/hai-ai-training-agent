Metric: Max Heart Rate (HRmax)

Formula:
HRmax = 208 − 0.7 × age

Source:
Tanaka H., Monahan K.D., Seals D.R. (2001)
Age-predicted maximal heart rate revisited.
Journal of the American College of Cardiology.

-----
HRV SDNN = Heart Rate Variability — Standard Deviation of NN intervals.

What that means

It measures how much your heart beat timing varies.

Your heart does NOT beat like:

1.00s
1.00s
1.00s

Instead it looks like:

0.92s
1.05s
0.98s
1.10s

SDNN is the standard deviation of those intervals.

Interpretation
HRV (SDNN)	Meaning
low (~20–40 ms)	stress / fatigue
medium (~40–70 ms)	normal
high (~70–120 ms)	well-recovered / good fitness
Why it matters for your system

Fatigue pattern usually looks like:

resting HR ↑
HRV ↓
sleep ↓
training load ↑

That combination is a classic overtraining signal used in sports science.

Paper reference

Shaffer & Ginsberg (2017)
An Overview of Heart Rate Variability Metrics and Norms.
Frontiers in Public Health.

Important for your project

Apple uses SDNN specifically, which is one of the standard HRV metrics used in physiology research.

So it’s scientifically legitimate to use in your fatigue detection engine.
